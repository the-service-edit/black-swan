Questions

Answered plainly.

If yours is not here, ask. The first conversation is free and without obligation.

How does working with a broker actually work?

We sit between you and the lenders. Rather than you approaching one bank at a time, we look across a panel of more than thirty lenders, find the products that suit your needs, and present your application with our recommendation. We handle the paperwork and the back-and-forth.

Why use a broker instead of going to my bank?

Your bank can only advise you on its own products. A broker compares a wide range of lenders and finds the one that genuinely fits, which is why most new mortgages in Australia are now arranged through a broker. It tends to save people time, and often money.

Do you charge a fee?

In most cases, no. We are generally paid a commission by the lender once your loan settles, so our advice costs you nothing. If a fee ever applies to your situation, we will tell you upfront and in writing, before any work begins.

My situation feels complicated. Can you still help?

Often, yes. Self-employed income, a first home with a modest deposit, an investment structure, a business purchase: these are where good advice matters most. The first conversation is free, and you will leave it knowing where you stand.

What do I need to bring to the first meeting?

Nothing. The first conversation is about understanding your goals, not assessing documents. If we go further together, we will give you a short, clear list of what is needed and help you gather it.
